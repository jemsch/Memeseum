Spongebob Memes

Evil Patrick

One fine day in the year 1999, an important episode of Spongebob Squarepants was released. Impacting millions, The season 1 episode given the title Nature Pants, holds the origin of a great meme known as the “Evil Patrick” meme. From this episode, arose a reaction image popular within recent years. A multitude of names are labeled to this phenomenon, including “Savage Patrick”, “Angry Patrick”, and “Malicious Patrick”, however, no matter the classification, the meme is most often interpreted as a way of expressing ones malicious thoughts during the acts of unlawful behavior. The usage of “Evil Patrick” is often times much less innocent than the original, virtuous scene containing the protagonist, Spongebob and his best friend, Patrick Star. The protagonist believes that he is a Jellyfish and Patrick Star attempts to capture Spongebob in an effort to make Spongebob accept who he is as a sea sponge. Some of the school appropriate examples of captions for this meme proudly provided by twitter includes, “Me kicking ice under the fridge” or “How my professor looks at his exam knowin it’s nothing like the study guide”, which essentially should provide the appropriate idea of how to take part in using this meme.

Bold and Brash

The exquisite painting created by the one and only Squidward aired during the year 2001 during the episode titled, Artist Unknown. The scene depicts Squidward presenting his work of art called “Bold and Brash”, to a critic who disagrees with Squidward’s enthusiasm and responds saying “More like belongs in the trash” while the painting is being thrown in the trash. The day September 29, 2013 is a crucial part of history, for that was the day the subreddit r/boldandbrash was created, which lead to the spread of the meme.

Mocking Spongebob

The episode this meme comes from is called Little Yellow Book. One of the most popular Spongebob memes comes from a great origin, a scene where the ever so intelligent Spongebob, behaves like a chicken. The screenshot posted by @OGBEARD on twitter contained the caption “How i stare back at little kids when they stare for too long”. Mocking Spongebob or “Spongemock” soon spread within the following days. The meme is properly used in a mocking tone, which can be expressed with an odd mix of uppercase and lowercase letters.

Spongegar

Whether it be Primitive Spongebob, Caveman Spongebob, or Spongegar, this meme portrays a glance of pure perfection and beauty. A primitive ancestor of Spongebob, introduced on December 31st 1999, during the episode SB-129 is part of the plot where Squidward travels back in time, leading him to meet an ancestor of Spongebob named Primitive Sponge. A new version of a prehistoric Spongebob was 1st aired in America on March 5, 2004, and is named Spongegar, who features in the episode Ugh. Spongegar is a more advanced version of Primitive Sponge who develops cooking skills, language, and use of clothes. However, their names were incorrectly switched by the media, causing the original Primitive Sponge to be called Spongegar as the use of this meme progressed. This meme is used as a reaction image resembling how no matter what the complexity a human’s brain is, basic desires that originate from ancestors continue to be a large portion of everyday life decisions. Spongegar is also often used as a sign of anger considering that the image implies a sense of aggression radiating from the character’s image.

Handsome Squidward

“Handsome Squidward” appears in the episode, The Two Faces of Squidward and as a painting in the episode, Chatterbox Gary. A graceful scene in The Two Faces of Squidward displays Handsome Squidward falling, which soon became a popular scene subject to editing into loops and remixes. One of the earliest uses of this meme was back in 2008 in the month of November when someone named SilverWingedBandit, created a loop of Handsome Squidward falling with the Mega Man 7 Intro Stage Theme playing over it.

*Breath in* Boi

This image, derives itself from a part of Spongebob Kah Rah Tay Contest, a Flash Game from Nick.com released in 2003. One of the earliest uses of this image comes from 4chan on December 22, 2015 and later got shared on tumblr, spreading the meme to other social media outlets and gaining popularity. Captions provided for this image are made to express the annoyance one would have when someone else is ignorant to obvious courses of action that could be taken with a higher degree of common sense.
